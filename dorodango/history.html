Dorodango is a traditional Japanese art form that involves transforming ordinary mud into polished spherical objects. The word "dorodango" can be translated to mean "mud dumpling" or "mud ball." The history of dorodangos is not well-documented, but it is believed to have originated in Japan many centuries ago. The exact origins of dorodango are unclear, as the art form was likely passed down through generations by word of mouth rather than being formally recorded. It is believed to have started as a pastime for children, who would create simple mud balls and then polish them to a glossy finish. Over time, dorodango evolved into a more refined and deliberate art form.

It began to gain recognition as a traditional craft in Japan during the 20th century, and its popularity has since spread to other parts of the world. Dorodangos are often admired for their simplicity and beauty. They come in various sizes and colors, depending on the type of soil or clay used in their creation. In recent years, dorodangos have gained attention as a form of meditative art, with people around the world experimenting with different techniques and materials to create their own unique mud balls.
